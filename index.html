Madame Marie Curie

The only woman to be honoured twice with Nobel Prize

Here's a time line of Madame Marie Curie's life:

  • 1867 - Born in Poland.
  • 1891 - She went to Paris to study at Sorbonne
  • 1895 - She married Pierre Curie, which was when her name became Marie Curie.
  • 1897 - She gives birth to her first daughter, Irene.
  • 1902 - While working on experiments, she discovered pure radium.
  • 1903 - Marie and her husband, Pierre, were awarded a Nobel Prize for their work with radioactivity.
  • 1904 - Marie was promoted to the chief assistant to her husband in his laboratory
  • 1904 - Pierre began teaching at Sorbonne.
  • 1906 - Marie's husband, Pierre, was killed in an accident. She took his place teaching at Sorbonne and was the first woman professor there.
  • 1911 - Marie won her second Nobel Prize, that time on her own, for her continued work in radioactivity.
  • 1914 - During WWI, Marie used her radioacitive reasearch materials to do "x-rays". The x-rays found bullets and other shrapnel in wounded soldiers.
  • 1922 - Marie became a member of the French Academy of Medicine. She Devoted herself to using her knowledge to help people.
  • 1929 - Marie was honoured everywhere she went, and was given a gram of radium as a gift.
  • 1932 - Marie and her sister started a reasearch facility to help fight cancer. It is still opened today and is called the Marie Sklodowska Curie Oncology Center.
  • 1934 - Marie got sick and died shortly after, but she will never be forgotten. Her work helped advance the medical world and save thousands of lives.
"Nothing in life is to be feared, it is only to be understood. Now is the time to understand more, so that we may fear less."

--Marie Curie
